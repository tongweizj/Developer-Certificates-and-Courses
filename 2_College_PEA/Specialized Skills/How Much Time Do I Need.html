What is Expected of Me?

                                 

Full-time studies require the same time commitment as a full-time job.

Most employees work 40 hours, and this does not include travel time. The EAP Program is a full-time, intensive English language program, so as an EAP student, you should aim to spend at least 40 hours of time on your studies.

Your total EAPP class hours with your professors, the two-hour lab class, and the one-hour seminar account for 22 hours. Therefore, you should aim for about 18 hours for homework and studying each week. In addition, if you think that certain skills are weak, you may have to plan to devote more time for that skill.

What if I have a part-time job?

Here is what York University here in Toronto advises students on its website, and this is for all students, not just for ESL students:

"Many students work part time while in school. Keep this in mind: a full course load (30 credits or 5 full courses during the Fall/Winter terms) is a full-time job, roughly equivalent to a 40-50 hour work week. Some students may be able to do an additional 10 hours of part-time work on top of this, but more than 10 hours would likely compromise your course work."

Where does my time go?

We all have 24 hours in a day. No one has more, and no one has less. What we accomplish depends on our circumstances, our approach, and our choices.

Analyzing How I Spend My Time

Time is like money in the bank. Know how much money there is to spend before spending it. In order to plan your study time, you need to know how much time you have available, and this will depend on your personal circumstances and situations.

Most of us cannot quickly change our life circumstances--married, with school age children, aging or sick parents, living far from the college, part-time jobs, recent newcomers just settling in, not living with spouses, etc. These situations often bring very time-consuming responsibilities.

How much time do you have for your studies? This is simple math:

[All my available time] minus [ time for required daily living activities] = time available for my studies

Use the questionnaire below to help you see how you generally spend your time each week. Consider the average amount of time you give to each activity. In some weeks, you will use more time and in some weeks, less, so give sufficient average time. When you answer all the questions, the box at the bottom will tell you how much time you have available for studying. Make a note of this number. Then print the completed survey.
